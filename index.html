How does this work?

Every heartbeat pushes a small wave of blood through the capillaries in your face. Your skin reddens by less than a percent with each beat — far below what your eye can resolve. MotionMag isolates that almost-invisible change and multiplies it until it crosses the threshold of human sight.

The pipeline runs entirely in your browser, in WebGL2:

  1. Pyramid. Build a 4-level Gaussian pyramid of every frame so we can pick the spatial scale that matches the signal (pulse lives at the coarse end; tremor lives near the original resolution).
  2. Bandpass. Run a per-pixel Butterworth biquad bandpass tuned to the cog's frequency band (0.8–2.5 Hz for the pulse cog). Everything outside that band — slow lighting drift, fast camera noise — gets thrown away.
  3. Amplify & reconstruct. Multiply what's left by α (default 50×) and add it back to the original frame. The 0.5% skin-color wobble becomes a 25% wobble; your heartbeat shows up on screen.

Based on Wu et al., MIT CSAIL 2012. Open DevTools → Network — nothing leaves your device.
